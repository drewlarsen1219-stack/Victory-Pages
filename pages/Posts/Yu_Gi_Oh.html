Cringe aside, the card game itself is fun. I enjoy games that have character building or, in this case, deck building. I love games that are strategic and artistic. The game requires you to read each card, which has the description on how the card works and its attack and defense points. Each card also comes with a piece of art that usually matches the card's effect and ATK/DEF points. Example here.

I have been playing this on my Game stick which I bought on Amazon. Mind you, this is an older version of the game and was made for the Gameboy originally. The newer versions of the game on the newer consoles I can only talk briefly on because they were too difficult and had too much bloat for my taste.

My plan is to figure out what the in between difficulty and game mode from the old gameboy games where you use the basic cards (Early Konami/Bandai Rules (Pre-2002) / Forbidden Memories ruleset) where the game is game is slow and grindy with very limited card effects, focus on simple Tributes and Fusion by type. And the hyper nerd level where you can play 5+ cards on your first turn and summon multiple monsters on the field easily. (Post-Link Era) Gemini's description: Link, Xyz, Synchro, Pendulum, and Fusion all used in complex, multi-step combos. Focus on ending the first turn with a massive "board" of protected monsters.

According to Google Gemini searching this up the middle ground that I have been curious about is the "GOAT Format" (Spring 2005) or "Edison Format" (Spring 2010). Where there is more strategy, powerful cards, but a steady pace. Gemini description: "Core Synchro/Xyz mechanics are introduced, but the game is still focused on 1-for-1 card advantage. The pace is strategic, not combo-driven."

Gemini: "It is named after the powerful card, Scapegoat, and the card pool is generally limited to cards released up to the set Invasion of Chaos and the ban list of April 2005. Key Features of GOAT Format: Focus on 1-for-1 Trades. Most turns involve playing one card to answer an opponent's one card (e.g., using Mystical Space Typhoon to destroy their set Spell/Trap). Limited Special Summoning: It relies mainly on Fusion and simple Effect Monster summons. There are no Synchro, Xyz, or Link Monsters. Tribute Monsters are King: High-Level monsters like Black Luster Soldier - Envoy of the Beginning and Chaos Emperor Dragon - Envoy of the End were the most powerful cards, requiring you to carefully manage your field to summon them. Skill is Positional: The game is won by superior resource management, knowing when to set a card versus playing it immediately, and positioning monsters for the next turn."
